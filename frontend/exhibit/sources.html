Scholastic & College

LIGHTHOUSE IN DIVISION 1 COLLEGE SOCCER

  1. “In his 41-year career as Temple soccer coach, he coached 36 All-Americans and earned 245 career victories.” Wikipedia, Pete Leaness, citing Temple Athletics Hall of Fame. en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pete_Leaness
  2. “Temple recognizes two national championships in men’s soccer under Leaness’ leadership: 1951 and 1953.” Temple Athletics, 2025 Men’s Soccer Record Book. owlsports.com/…/2025_Men_s_Soccer_Record_Book.pdf
  3. “Pete Leaness … Head Coach, Men’s Soccer, 1930–1970 … 36 All-Americans.” Temple Athletics Hall of Fame — Pete Leaness. owlsports.com/honors/hall-of-fame/pete-leaness/318
  4. “Five of us went on to play under full scholarships with Pete Leaness at Temple University, winning the Intercollegiate national title in my freshman year in 1951 … We won the National Title again in 1953 with roughly the same team.” Len Oliver, U.S. National Soccer Hall of Fame. Society for American Soccer History, 2015. ussoccerhistory.org
  5. “Jack Dunn … grew up in Philadelphia, where he played for the Lighthouse Boys Club … four-time All-American at Temple … head coach of Saint Joseph’s University men’s soccer for 17 seasons (1958–1975).” Wikipedia, Jack Dunn (soccer). en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_Dunn_(soccer)
  6. “Walter Bahr … coached at Frankford High School, Temple University (1971–1973), and Penn State (14 seasons).” Wikipedia, Walter Bahr. en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_Bahr
